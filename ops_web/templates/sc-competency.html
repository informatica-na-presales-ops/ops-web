Technical Acumen

Grasp the knowledge, experience, and desire needed to identify, qualify, and evaluate/scope the technical aspects of an opportunity and proficient in the CDW/DL journey. They are passionate towards technology, past, present, and future. They drive/influence conversations supporting technology to provide a solution. Independence and solution depth are primary factors to what drive this competency.

  • Unable to independently identify, qualify, or evaluate (i.e. demo, workshop, or POC) many of our solutions.
  • Very resource intensive to handle basic conversations around technology.
  • Narrow technology focused with very little desire to expand knowledge.
  • Does not have a strong grasp on the CDW/DL journey.
  • Proven delivery presenting the core Informatica platform overview.
  • Successfully achieves Presales onboarding certification.
  • Requires SME or peer support in order to deliver solution journey demonstrations.
  • Can perform most basic day to day tasks of role but may require additional coaching or assistance from peers and/or management. Tasks include but are not limited to orchestrating POCs, support workshops, responding to RFP/RFIs, performing high level discovery of core integration and cloud solutions.
  • Proven demonstration and certification in one Informatica journey area (e.g. Informatica Cloud DW/DL).
  • Can independently orchestrate the lifecycle of core platform integration and governance opportunities, including detailed technical discovery, orchestrating validation events such as multi-product POCs, leading workshops and knowledgeable on when and which SMEs and specialists to bring in.
  • Proven delivery of specific standard product demonstrations around Cloud DI and data governance.
  • Demonstration and certification in at least two Informatica journey areas (e.g. Informatica Cloud DW/DL & Data Governance).
  • Effectively whiteboard Informatica platform for multi-product opportunities.
  • Can successfully demonstrate at least two separate Informatica products with some customization.
  • Deliver Informatica solution architecture and demonstration that can address both an executive level as well as to an technical level audience that spans multiple products within the Informatica portfolio.
  • Proven thought leadership independence and professionalism in all interactions with customers peers and product management.
  • Can whiteboard the value of Informatica within the construct of a larger Ecosystem landscape.

Domain Knowledge

They have knowledge and experience in the role and understand the sales cycle and methodologies used. They have knowledge and experience in the Data Management and cloud Ecosystem space, both from a historical and future direction perspective. This is not about specific technology, it is about what drives the specific technology to be used. Understands ability and information that applies sales consulting as it pertains to the data management space.

  • Cannot articulate business and data management challenges with a customer.
  • Little to no experience with cloud ecosystems.
  • Unable or unwilling to build a rapport with the customer to become a trusted advisor.
  • Lacks an understanding of the role of sales consulting and the tasks that should be owned.
  • Unfamiliar with an understanding of the preferred sales cycle and the methodologies we use, and cannot define his/her ownership throughout.
  • Understands and can articulate the value and principles of Enterprise Data Management.
  • Is familiar with and can articulate cloud data warehouse concepts either on premises or as part of a cloud first or hybrid-cloud approach.
  • May still need assistance from peers and/or coaching from management in the following areas: developing trusted advisory status with clients, leveraging an effective strategy for developing rapport, proper use of sales methodology, and building credibility with clients.
  • Leverages industry knowledge coupled with an understanding of a customerʼs decision-making process, goals, strategies, and business objectives to achieve a high degree of technical win outcomes.
  • Technical advice and approach is considered consultative and sought by peers within their immediate sales region.
  • Follows sales best practices and demonstrates a clear strategy for gaining trusted advisorship with their clients.
  • Can understand and communicate the pros and cons of multiple architectural approaches to data management problems.
  • Achieves a single cloud ecosystem certification along with achieving Informaticaʼs 301 and 401 cloud certification.
  • Technical advice considered strategic and sought by others across different sales organizations.
  • Strong understanding of competitive landscape and can comfortably identify differentiation of our solutions and value in broader technical landscapes beyond Informatica.
  • Has achieved certification in at least two cloud ecosystems.
  • Brings practitioner level advice on data management principles and best practices regularly sought by multiple organizations within Informatica.
  • Mentoring and education on creative and new areas key for helping the sales organization drive pipeline and awareness.
  • Strong understanding of adjacent technology to the data management space including 3rd party on premises and cloud platforms, open source software, etc.
  • Certification in three or more cloud ecosystems.

Discovery and Qualification

Asks questions that promote trust and dialogue with the field and customers; encourages customer interaction through active listening to learn about the customerʼs problems before proposing product; communicates a customized solution using phrasing that expresses empathy and understanding of the customerʼs goals and needs.

  • Interaction with customer devoid of insight and awareness of customerʼs world.
  • No questions asked to explore customer needs and concerns.
  • Communication style non-consultative.
  • Conducts first level discovery of customer need for the identification of technical pains and outcomes.
  • Scope and depth of conversations are tactical and limited to basic use cases around one or two product areas.
  • Develops coherent technical requirements documentation.
  • Demonstrates solid listening skills and follow-up in customer engagements.
  • Prepared prior to customer visits and is able to shape communication strategy.
  • Coaches extended team on information learned.
  • Demonstrate agility to probe and drills-down to provide greater customer insight.
  • Uses phrases and terms from the customerʼs technical and business domain.
  • Uses paraphrasing and active listening to communicate empathy and awareness of customer concerns and needs.
  • Quickly develops rapport and trust with the customer.
  • Provides concise and detailed documentation to insure all stakeholders are in sync over the opportunity lifecycle.
  • Active listener, empathetic and motivational, articulates value tailored to audience.
  • Expert delivery of ROI, TCO mapped to customer pain.
  • Leverages sales processes, tools, and templates to gain insight into customerʼs world.
  • Can frame solution in the context of the customerʼs industry.

Teamwork and Collaboration

We work cooperatively across departments and geographies to achieve the best possible results for our customers, employees and shareholders. We understand the company goals and align our own goals and actions accordingly. This is a team sport. In order for us to be successful we each have to make the effort to ensure Informatica is a culture of “one team” with the competition being outside our walls, not within.

  • Territorial; weak communicator; competitive towards peers.
  • Confrontational or non-collaborative with field, peers, management, customers.
  • Minimal influence with others outside circle of authority.
  • Slow to share knowledge with others.
  • Responds to tasks and input when asked and offers feedback or advice when asked.
  • Actions and communications are limited to the immediate need therefore may miss the bigger strategy or longer term goal.
  • Inclusive; strong communication skills within and beyond immediate team.
  • Respects cultural and technical diversity.
  • Influences stakeholders of varying spheres of authority.
  • Recognized across various departments as trusted team player.
  • Mentors/influences others around them with their inclusive leadership style.
  • Proactively searches for opportunities to add value to the teamʼs success.
  • Places company and team interests above personal interests.
  • Influences and mentors key stakeholders internally and externally at every touch point across the Informatica customer life cycle (customer touch points include customer awareness of business problem, selection of best solution, order and contracts, implementation, training, support, and expansion).
  • Can mesh effortlessly into any team within or outside of their organization.

Leadership Skills

Leads by teaching others. Leads by example. Communicates often and sets high standards for oneself.

  • Never engages or contributes in team settings.
  • Never takes on responsibility to learn and teach beyond what is mandated.
  • Operates at a low standard “minimal viable” execution approach.
  • Demonstrated circle of influence tends to be limited to immediate sales team.
  • Influence skills are not yet fully developed and will offer input typically only when asked.
  • Focus is on immediate task and is not thinking of the needs of the broader team.
  • Demonstrated growth in sphere of influence beyond immediate sales team.
  • Proactively participates in team projects within their assigned sales area.
  • Shows initiative by proactively suggesting ideas to improve effectiveness of broader team.
  • Proactively owns their franchise and understands their business and opportunities.
  • Advice frequently sought for several areas of the Informatica platform and proactively seeks opportunities to lead independent projects for the broader SC community.
  • Has been able to effectively lead at least one team based project per year.
  • Is assigned as a mentor frequently for new hires within and/or outside their assigned region/area.
  • Is considered a model of behavior and actions for the Presales team.
  • Their advice and counsel are inspirational and creative and sought for on opportunities and situations across other sales teams.
  • They conduct their actions ethically and with humility.
  • Is expected to independently manage multiple small Presales teams for short term projects within a given year.

Communication

Ability to achieve desired change in audience behavior, attitudes, or perceptions through influential communication style as demonstrated in presentations, written communication (reports, emails, whitepapers, etc.), dialogue, conversations, meetings; negotiation skills.

  • Communication style is not tailored to audience motivators.
  • Inability to empathize with stakeholder needs, pain, business problems.
  • Is capable of communicating Informatica solutions at technical/functional level.
  • Both written and communication skills are clear but generally informational vs. influential.
  • Participates during internal planning discussions but may require prompting.
  • Leverages sales strategies and a more mature communication style to gather information leading to differing messages and value to their clients.
  • Operates more independently through proactive outreach and developing credibility with key technical stakeholders in their accounts.
  • Displays empathy to influence and articulate value to audience through active listening.
  • Can adjust message and style depending on audience while effectively handling objections.
  • Operates independently through proactive outreach and developing relationships with both business and technical stakeholders in their accounts.
  • Communication style viewed as expert level leading to gaining immediate credibility and long term relationships with both business and technical stakeholders.
  • Advises team on competitive stance and creates counter strategies to threats in accounts.
  • Can formulate overall technical account strategy e.g. SWOT analysis – strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
  • Publish at least 2 whitepapers or related capstone projects per year and keep public communications updated regularly (e.g. social media, blog posts etc.).

Planning and Prioritization

The individual ensures timely administrative tasks are completed. Executes at a high degree of time management. Focuses on critical and impactful workloads that contribute to the business. Assist in account and opportunity planning.

  • Activity tracking is not done in a timely fashion.
  • Expenses and other administrative tasks are often submitted late.
  • Rarely contributes to the business beyond minimal job related activities.
  • Generally low performance related to job duties.
  • No account or opportunity planning with sales counterparts.
  • Meets the minimum level of output on administrative tasks and opportunity management. More complex tasks such as orchestration of POCs may require some peer and management oversight.
  • Planning in general is sufficient but may lack details around unanticipated challenges or outcomes that may come up later.
  • Consistently maintains administrative tasks on time while providing more depth to expected outcomes (e.g. technical risk notes of current status and next steps).
  • Understands the impact and value of specific milestones of client engagements and can re-prioritize actions across multiple client opportunities.
  • Is capable of identifying blockers and can pivot to realign priorities with a focus on the end game of the specific customer engagement or project.
  • Efforts are guided by the end game in focus but also keenly aware of differences between tactical vs. strategic company goals.
  • Not willing to sacrifice a relationship over a short term gain if that is not the right solution for the customer.
  • Effective communicator of milestones and objectives for a variety of customer engagements (e.g. POCs).
  • Consistently achieves above average technical win due to their attention to detail on customer validation/POC events.
  • Views events, actions and impacts of decisions both internally and externally with a strategic lens.
  • Exceptional at orchestrating events supported by all key stakeholders both technical and business.
  • Consistently has a plan to anticipate challenges that may occur down the line.

Customer Advocacy

Build and maintain trusted relationships within our customer base. Collect customer experiences which can be used to drive our engagement and affect product roadmap. Act as an escalation point for customers.

  • Rarely does customer follow-up activity.
  • Limited to no engagement when other technical resources are engaged.
  • Never provides feedback to PM teams on customer needs.
  • Lacks awareness of customer implementation and value of INFA solutions.
  • Understands the importance of customer adoption of Informatica solutions but actions are more passive than proactive in nature.
  • Can perform basic follow up on any active support cases but will have limited influence on acceleration of resolution.
  • Has willingness to engage with CSM team.
  • Performs outreach to accounts on a regular cadence to insure adoption of Informatica solution by the customer.
  • Assigned customers in their territory are frequently achieving at or above the targeted adoption rate prescribed by the CSM organization and corporate leadership.
  • Collaborates with CSM team in handling proper hand offs at the end of a sales cycle.
  • Is able to identify and communicate customer issues and escalations with their immediate supervisor in a timely manner.
  • Highly consultative and views the customerʼs journey from a project point of view understanding the various gates they must achieve.
  • Introduces best practices to the discussion to help provide post sales adoption advice in conjunction with other Informatica SMEs.
  • Exceptional approach to insuring customer is able to meet key milestones for their adoption journey.
  • Is forward thinking in heading off potential customer challenges by analyzing their current approach.
  • Assigned customers in their territory are consistently achieving adoption targets excepting those situations that are beyond the control of the individual.
  • Proactively shares and communicates with product management on potential capabilities required by the customer and works to come up with a solution that has the customerʼs goals in mind.

Attitude

We encourage original thinking in all areas of our work. We set the highest standards and expectations for ourselves and strive to exceed them. This is not just about innovation or technical excellence. The idea that we create things weʼre passionate about — products, deployments, partnerships, this entire company — goes beyond any one single accomplishment. Having the right attitude goes to the core of everything that we do. It is an expression of how we feel about what we do and who we are when weʼre doing it.

  • Performs inconsistently and lacks clear vision and goals.
  • Operates at (or below) the bare minimum when engaged.
  • Reactively works on value added activities and projects.
  • Generally seen as a negative influence on others.
  • Performs basic tasks at minimum level of acceptance but are not considered innovative.
  • Enthusiastic about Informatica culture.
  • Engages others with an optimistic outcome.
  • Leans into situations without having to be prompted.
  • Willing to initiate actions without getting too far afield of goals and objectives as agreed upon by their manager.
  • Demonstrates curiosity for delving deeper into tasks and outcomes for continuous improvement of delivery and expertise.
  • Motivating in their behaviors and actions of themselves and in influencing their peers and colleagues.
  • Takes initiative on advanced technical solutions.
  • Learns from mistakes to avoid repeating behavior and shares outcomes with others.
  • Aggressively seeks knowledge on emerging trends and technology.
  • Proactively seeks higher learning and relevant data management disciplines and industry trends.

Corporate Citizenship

Develops and supports broader initiatives beyond individualsʼ goals. Supports company-wide decisions and executes. Develops material and collateral that benefits multiple facets of the business.

  • Never engages the team or broader INFA teams to drive initiatives.
  • Lacks the ability to provide content and thought leadership that is usable by others.
  • Never engages with other departments to drive new ideas or contribution to existing platforms.
  • Engages with immediate team but may require prompts for feedback.
  • Focus is on immediate requirements of opportunities and within the scope of their assigned territory.
  • Engages proactively with assigned teams and freely offers input and recommendations.
  • Understands the value to other teams with respect to lessons learned and customer successes and is willing to develop those and share when asked.
  • Frequently shares lessons learned and customer experiences outside of their immediate assigned territory.
  • Regularly is seen developing content for and helping on delivery to support field or corporate marketing initiatives.
  • Regularly shares lessons learned, best practices and solution ideas to multiple sales teams.
  • Consistently engages with broader teams across Informatica to build and deliver differentiated material and content in support of strategic corporate initiatives.
  • Viewed by the corporation as a reliable subject matter expert supporting corporate initiatives regularly putting high levels of trust in their advice and delivery.
